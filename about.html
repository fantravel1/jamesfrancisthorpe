About This Project

Honoring Jim Thorpe's Legacy

Our Mission

The James Francis Thorpe website exists to honor and preserve the legacy of the world's greatest athlete while educating new generations about his remarkable life, achievements, and the injustices he faced.

Jim Thorpe (Wa-Tho-Huk) was more than an athlete. He was a Native American icon who excelled at every sport he attempted, broke barriers in multiple fields, and maintained his dignity despite facing systematic discrimination. His story deserves to be told accurately, respectfully, and completely.

Why This Website Matters

  • Historical Accuracy: Presenting thoroughly researched, fact-based content about Thorpe's life
  • Cultural Respect: Honoring his Sac and Fox heritage and Native American identity
  • Educational Resource: Providing teachers, students, and researchers with reliable information
  • Correcting Injustice: Ensuring the true story of his medals and restoration is widely known
  • Inspiring Future Generations: Sharing how one person's excellence can transcend prejudice

What We Provide

This website offers:

  • Comprehensive biographical information
  • In-depth articles about specific aspects of Thorpe's life
  • Historical context for understanding his achievements
  • Resources for educators and students
  • Accurate information about the medal controversy and restoration

Our Commitment

We are committed to:

  • Maintaining the highest standards of historical accuracy
  • Respecting Native American culture and perspectives
  • Providing free access to educational content
  • Updating information as new research emerges
  • Honoring Jim Thorpe's memory with dignity and respect

Contact Us

We welcome questions, suggestions, and contributions to help improve this resource. Whether you're a researcher with new information, an educator seeking resources, or simply someone inspired by Jim Thorpe's story, we'd love to hear from you.

Contributing to the Project

If you have historical documents, photographs, or research about Jim Thorpe that you'd like to share, please contact us. We're particularly interested in materials that help tell the complete story of his life and achievements.

Acknowledgments

This website was created with deep respect for Jim Thorpe's memory and the Native American communities he represented. We acknowledge that Jim Thorpe was a citizen of the Sac and Fox Nation, and we honor the Indigenous peoples on whose traditional territories he lived and competed.

Special recognition goes to the Bright Path Strong campaign, led by Nedra Darling and Anita Thorpe, whose tireless efforts resulted in the full restoration of Jim Thorpe's Olympic titles in 2022.
